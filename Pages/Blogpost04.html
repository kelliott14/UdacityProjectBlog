blog post 04 | some visual studio shortcuts

There's somewhere between 1 million and 1 billion shortcuts for Visual Studio, here are a few of my favourites.

element dot class

We're punching out some code, we're killing it. Do we really have to type out the < and > the 'class='? Nope, here's your shortcut:

p.textpost [tab]

Which turns into

<p>class="textpost"></p>

easy.

doctype, html, meta?

No one likes starting on a blank document. It's intimidating. Also, no one remembers what needs to go in an html head tag, so what's the shortcut?

! [tab]

And that turns into:

leave a comment

Each language has a different way to comment text. And I will not remember them. Or I will at least not remember which language needs which symbols? You know who does remember? Visual Studio.

ctrl /

And that's it. Just type out your comment (single line, multiple lines), highlight it and hit ctrl /. VS will sort the rest.

Now go code!
