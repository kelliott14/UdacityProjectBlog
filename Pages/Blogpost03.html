blog post 03 | my frequently used apps when studying

Studying is hard, scrolling is easy. Here are some apps I use put the phone down, and keep it down!

focus keeper

Focus Keeper is an app that uses a Pomodoro timer to target your focus and to ensure you are resting too! The defaults are set to focus for 25mins, rest for 5, then on the 4th rest, it's a longer 20min one. Time flies when I'm using this app, I'm also standing up more often and giving my eyes a break from my screen more often. And, I'm also getting a lot of work done!

Available from the Apple store and from Google Play

toggl track

So you have some goals, some targets, you want to spend x amount of hours on study and x amount on work and x amount of homework and x amount on self learning? But how do track that? With Toggl Track! Easy to launch, easy to use, and the reporting? Also great! Toggl works with a login, so you can access your stuff through a browser too.

Available from the Apple store and from Google Play

forest

Ok so this one is just a bit cute. It's also a way to "lock" you out of your phone. Because once you've hit 'plant', if you try to do anything else with your phone, your little tree will die. Every tree ends up in your little Forest. A productive day never looked so good!

Available from the Apple store and from Google Play
